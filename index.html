Historical places in India

The Taj Mahal is one of the seven wonders of the world and is considered to be the most popular tourist attraction in the country. A symbol of love, the Taj was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for his queen Mumtaz. Tagore described the Taj as a "teardrop on the cheek of eternity". The monument attracts a lot of tourists all around the year.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Entry Fees : For Foreigners INR 1100 and for Indians INR 50


The Qutub Minar is at a height of 234 feet, this minaret is the tallest individual tower in the world and is a perfect display of Mughal architectural brilliance. The soaring and brave tower that allures tourists despite being destroyed by ravages of natural apocalypses several time. The monument attracts a lot of tourists all around the year.

Best Time to Visit: Throughout the year

Entry Fees : INR 100 for Foreigners and NR 30 for Indians


The Hawa Mahal stands at the intersection of the main road in Jaipur, Badi Chaupad. It is regarded as the signature building of Jaipur and was built by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. There is a small museum as well which holds some rich relics and miniature paintings. The monument attracts a lot of tourists all around the year.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Entry Fees : INR 200 for Foreigners and INR 50 for Indians


The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building, which is considered to be the pride of Kolkata. Built between 1906 and 1921, it is dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria. Now, it is a museum and a popular tourist spot under the Ministry of Culture.

Best Time to Visit: March and October

Entry Fees : INR 200 for Foreigners while INR 20 for Indians


The All India Gate War Memorial, popularly known as the India Gate, is a war memorial located in New Delhi. It is dedicated to the 82,000 soldiers, both Indian and British, who died during the First World War and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. The Amar Jawan Jyoti is the burning structure.

Best Time to Visit: February to April and August to November

Entry Fees : No Entry Fee Applicable


The Jallianwala Bagh is a place of great historical importance during the Indian struggle for Independence. It is a public garden in Amritsar which houses a memorial of national importance that was established by the government of India in 1951.

Best Time to Visit: November to March

Entry Fees : No entry fees applicable


The Sun temple dedicated to Lord Surya, this 13th-century temple is an architectural marvel and the quintessential example of Oriyan form of architecture. It is believed that the temple was constructed by King Narasimhadeva I of Eastern Ganga Dynasty. The shape of the temple is of a massive chariot with sumptuously engraved stone wheels, pillars and walls. A major part of the structure at present is in ruins. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site

Best Time to Visit: September to March

Entry Fees : INR 600 for Foreigners and INR 40 for Indians


The Red Fort was the official seat of Mughal rule and authority from 1648 onwards, when the 5th Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, decided to move the capital of the empire from Agra to Delhi. Constructed using red sandstone, it remains one of the architectural marvels of the Mughal era. In 2007, it became a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Formerly known as Quila-e-Mubarak or the Blessed Fort. It was a part of the medieval city of Shahjahanabad, popularly known today as 'Old Delhi'.

Best Time to Visit: October to February

Entry Fees : INR 500 for Foreigners and INR 20 for Indians


The Mysore palace also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical palace and a royal residence (house). It is located in Mysore, Karnataka. The former residence of the royal family of Mysore, the Mysore Palace is an extremely breath-taking example of the Indo-Saracenic style of architecture. It was built in the year 1912 for the 24th Ruler of the Wodeyar Dynasty and is till date one of the biggest palaces in the country. It is the well-known tourist attractions in India.

Best Time to Visit: Between the months of July and March

Entry Fees : INR 50 for foreigners and INR 50 for Indians
